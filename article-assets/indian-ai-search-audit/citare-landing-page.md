## Section 3 — Five findings preview

(Card grid, 5 cards. Each card = title + 1-paragraph teaser. Cards link to anchors in the full PDF or to the rikuq blog article.)

### Finding 1 — The most-cited domains aren't brand websites
When AI engines answer Indian buyer queries, what do they cite? In our top-20 most-cited list, the first 11 are all social platforms, marketplaces, aggregators, or one indie listicle blog. Zero brand-owned websites. The first brand site (sugarcosmetics.com) appears at position 12.

### Finding 2 — The 40-point gap
Brand-named comparison queries ("X vs Y") surface brands 73.3% of the time. Unbranded category queries ("best X for Y") surface them only 33.5%. The 40-point gap is where the actual AI-search opportunity lives. Brand search is won by default; unbranded search is the battle.

### Finding 3 — AIO and ChatGPT are different products
Per-brand surface rates vary by up to 80 percentage points between Google AI Overview and ChatGPT. Snitch surfaces in 10% of AIO cells but 90% of ChatGPT cells. Pristyn Care is the inverse: 80% AIO, 30% ChatGPT. Optimizing for one platform does not transfer to the other.

### Finding 4 — Series-A and B startups punch above weight
Cohort comparison: older brands (wipeout cohort) averaged 27% surface. Funded-not-leader brands averaged 46%. Newer-funded Series A/B startups averaged 49% — slightly higher than their more-established counterparts. AI search has no historical-authority moat.

### Finding 5 — A single indie listicle dominates a category
One independent blog (jimmyluxury.in) received 14 citations across the audit — more than any brand's own website, across any vertical. AI engines preferentially cite comprehensive listicle pages when answering category questions, and the brands listed inside those listicles get the surface mention.

## Section 8 — FAQ (with FAQPage schema for SEO)

**Q: What's in the PDF?**
> 30-40 pages of analysis: methodology, per-vertical findings, per-brand audit summaries, per-platform comparison charts, and the underlying citation graph data. Designed for marketing teams to brief their leadership on AI search strategy.

**Q: How do you measure AI search visibility?**
> Real browser sessions. We spin up actual instances of Chrome / Firefox and run queries through the public ChatGPT, Google AI Overview, Gemini, Claude, and Perplexity interfaces — not their APIs. The result matches what a real user typing the same query would see. APIs return different responses than the public interfaces; for citation measurement, the public interface is what matters.

**Q: How was the audit sample chosen?**
> 25 prospects across 16 verticals, weighted toward Indian consumer brands (D2C beauty, D2C apparel, healthcare, fintech, real estate, hospitality, insurtech, jewellery) and Indian SaaS (logistics, payroll, CRM, certification, coaching). The sample is curated for vertical breadth + brand recognizability, not statistical representativeness of every Indian brand.

**Q: Why was the audit done in 72 hours?**
> AI search retrieval shifts weekly. We compress audits into short windows so the comparison is apples-to-apples. Quarterly republication will show how surface rates evolve.

**Q: Can my brand be audited?**
> Yes. Citare runs single-brand audits using the same methodology. Reach out via [citare.ai](https://citare.ai/contact) for a quote.

**Q: Is the underlying data available?**
> The aggregate data is in the free PDF. Per-cell raw data is available under NDA to clients of Citare's audit service.

## Implementation notes

- **Email capture**: use Brevo (your existing newsletter tool) or a simple custom form posting to a Cloudflare Worker that adds to the Brevo list. The list should be tagged "indian-ai-search-audit" for segmentation.
- **Welcome email**: automated welcome email with the PDF download link + a quick "what to expect from rikuq + Citare" intro. Goes out within 30 seconds of signup.
- **PDF hosting**: store the PDF in Cloudflare R2 or in citare.ai's static assets. Public URL or signed-URL (less leakage if you want to keep tighter control).
- **OG tags**: hero image = the same chart from Slide 3 of the LinkedIn carousel. Description = "First published audit of how AI search treats 25 Indian brands. Free PDF + per-brand data."
- **Schema markup**: WebPage + FAQPage (from Section 8) + Article (for the source blog post).
- **Tracking**: GA4 events for: page-view, scroll-depth (25%/50%/75%/100%), form-view, form-submit, PDF-download. Plausible goal: form_submit.
- **Mobile responsive**: critical. Most LinkedIn-driven traffic will be mobile.
