## Body

```
I run Citare (citare.ai), an AI search visibility tool. As a side-project to client work, we just finished a 25-brand audit across all 5 major AI search platforms (ChatGPT, Google AI Overview, Gemini, Claude with browsing, Perplexity).

500 queries. 777 citation URLs captured. 72-hour audit window.

The result is the first published research on this topic I've been able to find — at least for Indian brands. And the findings rewrite what most Indian marketing teams should be optimizing for.

**The headline finding:**

The domains AI engines actually cite on Indian buyer queries are NOT brand websites. The first brand-owned site in our top-20-cited list appears at position 12. The top 11 are:

1. Instagram (21 citations)
2. jimmyluxury.in — one indie SEO listicle blog (14)
3. YouTube (12)
4. Practo (11)
5. Google Play Store reviews (11)
6. Reddit (10)
7. MagicBricks (9)
8. JustDial (9)
9. Amazon.in (9)
10. Policybazaar (8)
11. tryreadable.ai (7)

The brand-owned site that finally appears at position 12 is sugarcosmetics.com (7 citations). It out-cites every other brand site in our corpus by 1-2 citations.

For context: most Indian brand teams are spending the vast majority of their content budget on owned-property SEO. The data is showing those pages are being read past in favor of social, aggregator, and third-party listicle content.

**Other findings worth quoting:**

- Brand-named comparison queries ("Brand X vs Brand Y") surface 73% of the time. Unbranded category queries ("best X for Y") surface 33%. A 40-point gap.

- Per-brand asymmetry between AIO and ChatGPT goes up to 80 percentage points (Snitch: 10% AIO surface, 90% ChatGPT). Optimizing for one platform doesn't transfer to the other.

- Newer-funded Series A/B startups average 49% surface rate. Funded-not-leader brands (Series B+) average 46%. The wipeout cohort (older brands) averages 27%. The gap is funding, not age — AI search has no historical-authority moat.

- One indie SEO listicle blog (jimmyluxury.in) was cited 14 times across the audit — more than any single brand's own website got, in any vertical. AI engines preferentially cite comprehensive listicles when answering category questions.

**What I'd love feedback on from the IH community:**

1. If you've done your own AI search visibility audit (or run similar tools), how does our methodology hold up?

2. For non-Indian markets (US, EU, SE Asia), are you seeing the same "AI cites third-party surfaces over brand sites" pattern, or is this primarily an Indian-market dynamic?

3. The most common pushback I've heard so far is "well, this is a sample of 25 brands, not statistically representative." Fair criticism. But what would convince you the pattern is real? Quarterly republication? Larger sample? Open-sourcing the per-brand data?

**Disclosure:** I built one of the measurement tools used in the audit (Citare). I named all 25 brands in the full report and explicitly say where each one wins and loses, so the methodology is defensible whether you're using Citare or any competitor.

Happy to AMA in the comments. Also building a series of follow-up posts unpacking each finding — let me know which finding you'd want unpacked first and I'll bump that to the top of the queue.
```
